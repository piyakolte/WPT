    <h1>Diwali</h1>

    <h2>Introduction</h2>
    <p>Diwali, also known as Deepavali, is a major Hindu festival celebrated in India and around the world. 
       It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.</p>

    <h2>History</h2>
    <p>The festival has various historical origins, including the return of Lord Rama to Ayodhya after defeating Ravana, as described in the Ramayana. 
    It also marks the day Lord Krishna defeated the demon Narakasura.</p>

    <h2>Celebrations</h2>
    <p>Diwali is celebrated with various rituals, including the lighting of oil lamps (diyas), fireworks, and the worship of deities like Lakshmi and Ganesha. Families clean their homes and decorate them with rangoli and lights.</p>

    <h2>Regional Variations</h2>
    <p>While Diwali is celebrated across India, it has regional variations. In northern India, it often coincides with the harvest festival, while in southern India, it marks the victory of Lord Krishna.</p>

    <h2>Significance</h2>
    <p>Diwali represents hope, joy, and the triumph of light over darkness. It encourages charity and togetherness among communities.</p>
